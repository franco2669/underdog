## 1a. Happy Endings (already-adopted dogs)

These are the rescues that prove the underdog frame works. They are the editorial spine of the site. Mercy, Rosa, and Betty are the priority three. If you have 5-7 more in the same vein, even better.

For each happy-ending dog, please send:

- [ ] **Name**
- [ ] **Breed, age at intake, sex**
- [ ] **The intake story.** Where did you find them, what condition were they in, what did the first week look like? Voice memo is great — 2-5 minutes per dog, just talk.
- [ ] **The rehab.** What was hard, what worked, what didn't. The sugar-rub detail and the box-in-the-rain detail are exactly what we need. The specifics ARE the story.
- [ ] **Where they are now.** Where the dog landed, the family, how the placement is going years later. If you have an updated adopter quote, attach it (or note it in section 2 below).
- [ ] **What they were counted out for.** What did others say should happen to this dog? Vet recommended amputation, shelter recommended euthanasia, previous rescue called them "unadoptable" — whatever it was. The underdog frame lives in this answer.
- [ ] **Photos.** 4-8 per dog. Mix of: a tight portrait of their face, a full-body shot, one "before" intake photo if you have one (this is gold even if it's hard to look at), one "now" with the adopter family, and one candid. Phone photos are fine. Send the originals, not screenshots.

**Priority happy endings to start with:**
1. Mercy (the box-in-the-rain story)
2. Rosa (the sugar-rub / no-amputation story)
3. Betty (the aggressive mastiff who got placed anyway)

## 1b. Currently available dogs (1-3 to start)

This is a different content shape. We're not telling the past-tense rescue arc here. We're telling the truth about who this dog is **right now** and what kind of person could take them home. We'll build one Available Dog profile in Phase 2 to anchor the template, then add more as they cycle through.

For each currently available dog you'd like featured first, please send:

- [ ] **Name**
- [ ] **Breed, age, sex, weight, current adoption status**
- [ ] **How they came to you.** Brief, present-tense. ("We pulled her from a shelter in San Bernardino last month, she'd been there for 11 days.")
- [ ] **Who this dog is.** Personality, energy, what makes them themselves. Not breed stereotypes — this dog specifically.
- [ ] **Where they are in rehab.** What's been done, what's still being worked on. If they're ready to go home, say so.
- [ ] **Who can adopt this dog.** Required experience level, household setup (yard? no kids under 10? no other dogs?), commitment to ongoing training if applicable.
- [ ] **Who can NOT adopt this dog.** Be blunt. First-time owners, apartments with thin walls, families with toddlers — whatever applies. This is the filter, and it IS the brand.
- [ ] **Photos.** 4-8 per dog. Same mix as above, but emphasis on "current state" photos — how they look and act today.

**Pick 1-3 dogs to start with.** We'll use one of them as the template anchor in Phase 2 and add the rest as we go. Your call which ones — pick whoever needs the most help finding a home right now.

## 3. Volunteer asks (specific, current)

The single biggest fix to the Volunteer page: stop being vague. We need a list of what you actually need this month or this quarter.

For each open role, please send:

- [ ] **Role name** (e.g. "Saturday morning dog walker")
- [ ] **Where** (your home? a partner facility? on the road?)
- [ ] **When** (specific days/times if recurring; "as needed" if not)
- [ ] **How many people you need**
- [ ] **What experience is required** (none, some, must be comfortable with large breeds, etc.)
- [ ] **How to get started** (just show up? phone call first? trial shift?)

Examples of the level of specificity we want:
- *"We need 4 people for Saturday morning walks, 9-12, in Riverside. No experience needed; we'll train. Text Shoshi."*
- *"We need someone with a pickup truck for transport runs once or twice a month, mostly to Banning and back. Must be insured."*
- *"We need 1-2 people who can foot-soak skin-condition dogs at our location, weekdays 5-7pm. Some experience preferred."*

A list of 5-10 of these is gold. Even 3 will transform the Volunteer page.

## 4. Donation impact numbers (real costs)

You said people don't believe donations matter. The way we fix that is concrete numbers tied to real dogs. Please share, even rough:

- [ ] **What does a typical month of food cost** for one large-breed dog in your care?
- [ ] **What does a routine vet visit cost?** (vaccines, basic check)
- [ ] **What does an emergency vet visit typically cost?** (range is fine: "between X and Y")
- [ ] **What did Rosa's skin treatment cost total?** (Approximate is fine.)
- [ ] **What does a parvo intake cost?** (Approximate.)
- [ ] **What's your monthly fixed overhead** (boarding partners, transport, supplies)?
- [ ] **What's your current monthly donor count, roughly?** And what would a 50% increase look like in real terms?

We'll turn these into specific donation tiers: "$25/mo covers a week of food for one dog." That's what converts; vague gratitude doesn't.
